One Piece

Plot

Monkey D. Luffy is a young man made of rubber who, inspired by his childhood idol, the powerful pirate Red-Haired Shanks, sets out from the East Blue Sea to find the mythical treasure, the One Piece, and proclaim himself King of the Pirates. In order to organize his own crew, the Straw Hat Pirates, Luffy rescues and befriends a pirate hunter and swordsman named Roronoa Zoro, and the two embark on a quest for the titular treasure. Nami, a money-obsessed thief and navigator; Usopp, a sniper and compulsive liar; and Sanji, a perverted but chivalrous cook,join them on their journey.

Legendary Music

The anime television series currently has 41 theme songs, 23 opening themes, and 18 ending themes. Beginning with episode 279, ending themes were dropped, and beginning with episode 326, opening themes were increased from 110 seconds to 150 seconds. The opening and ending themes were dubbed into English by various voice actors in episodes 1-206 of Funimation's English-language release of the series, before reverting to the Japanese versions from episodes 207 onwards, and later some openings were not licensed by Funimation's release, leaving only the narration dubbed on select opening themes.

    • also known as "Straw Hat Luffy" and simply "Straw Hat," is the main character of the manga and anime series One Piece. He is the founder and captain of the infamous and powerful Straw Hat Pirates, as well as one of their best fighters. He wants to find the legendary treasure left behind by the late Gol D. Roger and thus become the Pirate King, which would help him realize an unknown dream he has only told his brothers and crew about. He believes that being the Pirate King entails having the greatest amount of freedom in the world.
  • Roronoa Zoro
    • also known as "Pirate Hunter," is a Straw Hat Pirates combatant, one of their two swordsmen, and one of the Straw Hat Grand Fleet's Nine Senior Officers. He is the second member of Luffy's crew and the first to join it, joining during the Romance Dawn Arc. Zoro is one of the Straw Hats' three most powerful combatants, alongside Luffy and Sanji, as a master of Three Sword Style, a swordsmanship style he developed during his childhood training in Shimotsuki Village. His ambition is to become the greatest swordsman in the world, fulfilling a promise he made to his late childhood friend Kuina.

  • Vinsmoke Sanji
    • is the Straw Hat Pirates' cook and one of the Straw Hat Grand Fleet's nine senior officers. He is the crew's fifth member and the fourth to join at the end of the Baratie Arc. He disowned his family twice, once as a child and again after reuniting with them as an adult. He was born as the third son and fourth child of the Vinsmoke Family (thus making him a former prince of the Germa Kingdom). After fleeing the Vinsmokes as a child, he eventually ended up in Zeff's care as the Baratie's sous chef, where he stayed until he met Monkey D. Luffy, who convinced him to join his crew.His ambition is to discover All Blue, the rumored chef's paradise where East Blue, West Blue, North Blue, and South Blue meet, along with their wildlife. Along with Luffy and Zoro, he is one of the Straw Hats' top three fighters.
  • Tony Tony Chopper
    • Chopper, also known as "Cotton Candy Lover," is the doctor of the Straw Hat Pirates and one of the Straw Hat Grand Fleet's nine senior officers. He is the crew's sixth member and the fifth to join at the end of the Drum Island Arc. During the Long Ring Long Land Arc, he was temporarily forced to join the Foxy Pirates, but was quickly returned to Luffy's crew. Chopper is a reindeer who consumed the Hito Hito no Mi, a Devil Fruit that allows its user to transform at will into a human hybrid or a human. He was born on Drum Island and was raised by his two paternal figures, Doctors Hiriluk and Kureha.
  • Nico Robin
    • She is the archaeologist of the Straw Hat Pirates and one of the Straw Hat Grand Fleet's nine senior officers. She is also known as the "Devil Child" and the "Light of the Revolution." She is the crew's seventh member and the sixth to join at the end of the Arabasta Arc. She left the crew briefly during the Water 7 Arc, but returned during the Enies Lobby Arc.Robin consumed the Hana Hana no Mi at a young age, endowing her with the ability to duplicate her body parts (or her entire body) on any surface at will. As the sole survivor of the destroyed West Blue island Ohara, she is one of only two people in the world known to be able to read and decipher Poneglyphs, a forbidden and dangerous skill to the World Government.As "Miss All Sunday,"[1] she served as the vice president of Baroque Works, serving and collaborating directly with the organization's president, the ex-Warlord: Crocodile, who operated under the codename "Mr. 0." Before joining the Straw Hats, she was a secondary antagonist in the Arabasta Saga. Her ambition is to discover the Rio Poneglyph, which reveals the true history of the world, particularly the Void Century.
  • Franky "Cutty Flam"
    • is the Straw Hat Pirates' shipwright and one of the Straw Hat Grand Fleet's nine senior officers. He is the eighth member of the crew and the seventh to join at the end of the Post-Enies Lobby Arc.He chose to go by his nickname "Franky" until eventually permanently abandoning his true name at Iceburg's request to conceal his identity. Franky, originally from South Blue, was abandoned by his family when he was four years old, eventually making his way to Water 7. There, he became a member of Tom's Workers until an incident severely damaged his body, forcing him to augment himself into a cyborg. When he returned to Water 7, he became the leader of the Franky Family, a gang of shipwrecked and bounty hunters.At the start of the Water 7 Arc, Franky and his followers were enemies of the Straw Hats, but circumstances forced them to become allies at the end of the same arc and the Enies Lobby Arc. To fulfill his dream of building a ship and circumnavigating the world, Franky built the Thousand Sunny and joined the Straw Hat Pirates.
  • Soul King Brook
    • is the Straw Hat Pirates' musician, one of their two swordsmen, and one of the Straw Hat Grand Fleet's nine senior officers. He is the crew's ninth member and the eighth to join at the end of the Thriller Bark Arc. Brook consumed the Yomi Yomi no Mi, which allowed him to resurrect once. Brook eventually learned to tap deeper into his Devil Fruit's powers, giving him significant control over his own and other people's souls.He was a member of the Rumbar Pirates before dying and being resurrected by the power of the Yomi Yomi no Mi. However, due to the length of time it took for his soul to return to his body, it was reduced to a skeleton, with only his afro remaining. Brook drifted alone in the Florian Triangle for 50 years before meeting Luffy and becoming an ally of the Straw Hats during the Thriller Bark Arc before officially joining the crew. His ambition is to meet up with an old friend, Laboon, at Reverse Mountain, where he lives with Crocus.
